Summary: Latitude vs. Cloudiness



Cloudiness does not seem to have a strong correlation with Latitude, meaning as one gets closer or further to equator, the levels of cloudiness may be dependant on other factors (closeness to ocean). Continents tend to be less cloudy than adjacent oceans (at the same latitude), except in the equatorial belt. On average, cloudiness is highest around 60-S (about 80% of the time) but there is only 40% cloud cover at the South Pole. These figures reflect the ascent and subsidence occurring at these respective latitudes, and the large amount of ocean at around 60-S. Satellite-based cloudiness estimates over surfaces covered by snow or ice are somewhat uncertain, especially at night.
